**Source**: SMU (Southern Methodist University), BU (Boston University), MGH (Massachusetts General Hospital) exposure therapy programs + Stefan Hofmann's CBT materials

## Embarrassment-Induction Exposures
- Stand at Starbucks, tell jokes loudly in front of whole café
- Buy pastry, take one bite, ask for a different one
- Drop drink in store, let water go everywhere, don't clean it up
- Wear silly attire (3-D glasses, funny hat, backward/inside-out shirt, Santa hat, construction paper crown) in crowded stores
- Male participant: try on half-unbuttoned shirt, ask strangers "does this fit?"
- Enter restaurant for 2, look at menu, tell waitress "I'm being stood up"
- Return embarrassing items to stores (Kama Sutra, Joy of Sex — ask staff opinion, have long conversation, then return)
- Ask strangers about obvious locations while standing right outside them ("Where is Fenway Park?" at Fenway)
- Walk backwards slowly on crowded street for 3 minutes
- Wear shirt backward, inside-out, misbuttoned — goal: make eye contact with 3 people

## Rejection / Conflict Exposures
- Cancel hotel romantic package after long concierge conversation (massage, carriage ride, tickets) — no reason, just changed mind
- Call Borders, reserve non-existent book, argue with employee, waste their time
- Hold mock job interview with hostile employer (frowns, disagrees with everything, "you're not qualified")
- Ask cheesy pick-up lines from strangers
- Go to every table in crowded restaurant asking for "Joe Smith"
- Try to purchase items without sufficient money
- Ask 3+ strangers in subway for $20
- Ask businesses for under-the-table payment, request to speak with manager

## Phone Exposures
- Make speakerphone calls in front of group with ridiculous requests:
  - Call MBTA asking for bus stop outside their house ("I wear stilettos")
  - Call airport about made-up flight status, insist information is correct
  - Call Barnes & Noble: place The Gas We Pass, Everybody Poops, and chocolate chip cookie on hold
  - Call airport: how much liquid allowed, want to bring 2 gallons of milk/OJ
- Call restaurants asking random info: alligator soup on menu? chef quality? chef training years?
- Call bookstores asking if they carry books about poop
- Persuade cold-call businesses to buy a product (real sales practice)
- Clipboard survey about construction project, last question: "better boys name, Howard or Kenneth?"

## Social Performance Exposures
- Sing/dance in street or subway wearing attention-grabbing clothing
- Karaoke in public
- Formal PowerPoint presentation in front of audience
- Acting out a play
- Speak one-on-one with confederates → mock dinner party
- Mock birthday party at bar: tell embarrassing stories, make toasts, cause accidents
- Sing Happy Birthday solo to a group
- Sing Jingle Bells in streets wearing Santa/elf hats
- Go to bars during peak Friday happy hour, initiate conversations
- Coffee date with confederate of opposite sex

## Everyday Social Exposures
- Hand out "have a nice day" cards at mall, especially to people avoiding you
- Approach strangers in stores and start conversations
- Ask security to walk you to car, then spend minutes rummaging saying "oops, not here"
- Mock job interview (standard, not hostile)
- Small talk in grocery stores, subway, coffee shops (switch cars at each stop)
- Administer survey to many people in transit
- Use public restrooms (anxiety-provoking for some)
- Sit down with strangers in coffee shops
- Participate in mock dinner party with confederates
- Walking through grocery store wearing attention-grabbing attire, talking to customers/employees, mumbling while talking, purposely having accidents (spilling, dropping)
