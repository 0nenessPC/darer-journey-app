**Source**: CBT group therapy protocol for social anxiety (public speaking + in-vivo exposures, Boston-area locations)

## Public Speaking Exposures — Group Sessions (Weeks 3-7)

### Personal / Vulnerability Topics
- Wedding toast (bring drinking glasses)
- Embarrassing story / worst date story / first kiss
- Joke telling — dirty jokes, really bad jokes
- Self-promotion: bring artwork/photos, audience acts as gallery owners
- Talk about ideal job and why they don't have it

### Mock Job Interviews
- One doctor plays authority figure
- Standard job interview questions

### Debate / Controversial (take opposite opinion)
- Teaching swing dancing, tap dancing, square dancing
- Acting out Romeo & Juliet scenes with group member
- "Men should make more money than women" (flip if needed)
- "Why kids at age 10 should be having sex"

### Performance
- Playing guitar in front of group
- PowerPoint presentations
- Singing Happy Birthday / Jingle Bells to each group member
- Group singing with therapists + confederates
- Speed-dating simulation (5 min rotations with confederates)
- Gettysburg address recitation

### Interoceptive + Speech Combo
- Jumping jacks + hyperventilating before speaking

### Imperfection / Perfectionism Exposures
- Messing up presentation on purpose
- Imperfect speech with lots of pauses
- Boring presentation
- Give wrong facts
- Disordered monologue (10-sec pauses with eye contact)
- Incorrectly conjugate verbs, use swear words

### Small Talk + Phone
- Small talk with confederate while group observes 1x1 interaction going poorly (confederate gives one-word answers, lets gaps hang)
- Call fancy restaurants: "why don't you sell sushi?", "is all your food organic?"
- Call someone they haven't called in a long time (in front of group)
- Speakerphone calls in the room
- Call CA government offices

### Academic / Cognitive Challenge
- Math: long division (need calculator), stress counting task (MMSC)
- Writing while being observed
- Company pitch proposal + audience gives criticism

### Sex Education Exposure
- Field embarrassing questions from confederates (Go Ask Alice)
- What to do to contract an STD, STDs PowerPoint
- Step-by-step hemorrhoid instructions (confederates ask questions)
- Props: big paper with diagrams, cucumber, condoms, hemorrhoid cream + picture

### Birthday Party Exposure
- Toast making, cake/candles, balloons, group signs card
- Props: masks (worried about H1N1), pizza, crate paper

### Compliment Reception
- People stare and give compliments → respond with "Thank you"

## In-Vivo Exposures — Real-World Locations

### Whole Foods
- Talk to 5+ strangers, ask 3+ questions each (goal: not "perfect" conversation, just do it)
- Identify which types of people are most anxiety-provoking
- Spill coffee/water
- Ask cheese counter recommendation → tell them it's bad
- Add hats/gowns (attention-grabbing)
- Ask obvious/dumb questions
- Challenge customers about calories in mac and cheese, ask for manager about caloric intake
- "I'm taking a photography class, can I take your picture?" (in front of store)
- Ring Salvation Army bell with Santa hat
- Ask manager why they don't sell Coke products
- Criticize customers at salad bar for their choices
- Tell Customer Service the store is dirty
- Tell sushi bar that uncooked fish will make people sick
- Order coffee, take sip, ask for money back (or pizza)

### CVS — Embarrassing Item Returns
- Must ask questions or explain why returning
- Return items with wrong receipts (each bought separately)
- Items: adult diaper (XXL), sexual potency product, hemorrhoid cream, Vagisil, diarrhea product, tampons, yeast infection treatment, XXL condoms, enemas (3-pack)
- Ask pharmacist: "doctor gave me Prep H but I heard sitting in Clorox is better — what do you think?"
- Sing in the store

### Liberty Hotel — Gallery/Event Night
- Complain about lack of vegan options at restaurant (The Clink)
- Criticize the art
- Ask about menu/wines, don't order anything
- Take pictures of people
- Ask to talk to manager
- Negotiate demanding honeymoon package → go back and return it

### Subway / T Stations / South Station / Harvard Square
- Hand out Halloween candy
- Survey: talk to 10 people, complete whole survey with each
- Be pushy in crowds
- Ask to take people's pictures
- Get petition signatures
- Ask for someone's seat when other empty seats available
- Share personal info + get opinions on controversial topics → give opposite opinion on ride back
- Disagree with what people say
- South Station: take pictures, sing songs, read poems, get signatures, wear crazy hats
- Harvard Square: talk to Psychology professors, approach students in Harvard Yard, outdoor bar at Charles Hotel (Noir Bar), career center, skaters in the Pitt
- Pronounce hard words, ask strangers what they mean → disagree with their definition, make up own

### Faneuil Hall
- Talk to strangers, recite poems, sing songs
- Bar hopping (Ned Devine's, Cheers): ask bartender lots of questions, leave saying beer selection is too limited
- Barter: order coffee, say only have 50 cents, offer to bring $10 later
- Help street performers
- Try on clothes, don't buy anything, or use incorrect/expired coupon
- Present ABCT poster data
- Go into bars, practice toast at tables
- Stand in line, talk to people, go to back of line when at front (looking weird)
- Sit at tables of people already eating
- Victoria's Secret: ask for best recommendation, share "favorites"
- Talk to manager, interrupt larger group eating together
- Ask for discounts
- Draw bad caricatures for free (crayons + sketchpad)
- Walk around with shirt inside-out, tag still on, ask strangers how much they paid

### Dick's Last Resort
- Reserve birthday table (shy person → request meanest servers)
- Order appetizers/dessert, sing Happy Birthday, be loud ("Snap, Crackle, Pop")
- Send back food or drink
- Ask lots of questions about dinner, don't order
- Wear hats in Faneuil Hall after

### Pub Crawl (TD Bank Garden area)
- Bars: Harp (85 Causeway St), Canal Street, The Greatest Bar (262 Friend St)
- Beg for Celtics tickets
- Argue with/interview Celtics fans (Cavaliers better, LeBron better than KG)
- Go to bar with dress code wearing wrong clothes
- Happy hour with business people (Splash)
- Ask people to buy you a drink or give up their bar seat

### Other In-Vivo Ideas
- Caroling on Charles St / Boston Common
- Apply to McDonald's/Starbucks, ask to be paid under the table
- No 9 Park, 75 Chestnut, Enoteca: go without reservation, fight for table, ask lots of questions
- Order Starbucks, return it
- Academic talks: MIT, Harvard, Boston Public Library, Borders, career fairs
- Karaoke: machine, CDs, song list, rating form, microphones, pizza/salad/soda, certificates
